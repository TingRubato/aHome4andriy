Anonymous Supporters

To those who gave quietly—
Your anonymity mirrors the way Andy would have wanted to be remembered: not for the attention, but for the ripples of care he created. You understood this instinctively.

We lost Andy, and in losing him, we discovered something about the mathematics of love—how it doesn't divide when shared, but multiplies in unexpected ways.

Your contribution wasn't charity. It was participation in the ancient human practice of making meaning from loss, of refusing to let someone's impact end with their breathing.

There's something profound about strangers choosing to honor a person they may never have met. In giving to Andy's memory, you became part of his story's continuation—not its ending, but its transformation into something that touches lives he'll never know he's touching.

The bench that Andrew and Misha are creating will hold more than bodies seeking rest. It will hold the weight of your collective decision that some people deserve to be remembered in wood and shade, in the quiet moments when someone sits down and feels, without knowing why, less alone.

This is how we fight the finality of death: not by denying it, but by insisting that the love someone generated keeps moving through the world, finding new homes, creating new connections, inspiring new acts of tenderness.

Andy's celebration of life became our education in living—in how to transform absence into presence, ending into beginning, grief into the fierce determination to honor what mattered about a person by becoming more of what mattered about ourselves.

We will tend this memorial like a garden, knowing that what grows there isn't just trees and benches, but the proof that one person's light can illuminate paths they'll never walk.
